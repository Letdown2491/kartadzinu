Overview

Kartadzinu is a constructed Romance language representing the fictional evolution of Latin in North Africa. Named for Carthage, the great city refounded by Rome, Kartadzinu developed with significant influence from the Berber (Amazigh) languages of the region. Unlike the Romance languages of Europe, it preserves features lost elsewhere while developing unique innovations.

Bona vja! Kom stas?
Hello! How are you?

Key Features

  • Aspect-prominent verbal system — Distinguishes perfective (completed) from imperfective (ongoing) actions
  • Construct state — Possessive suffixes attach directly to nouns for inalienable possession
  • VSO word order — In narrative and formal registers
  • Berber influence — Borrowed vocabulary and grammatical features
  • Betacism — The b/v merger characteristic of African Latin
  • Inclusive/exclusive "we" — nos (exclusive) vs nosin (inclusive)

Featured Translation

Tard te amai, belesa tan antika e tan noba, tard te amai!
"Late have I loved you, beauty so ancient and so new, late have I loved you!"
— Augustine of Hippo, Confessions X.27 (c. 397 AD)

Augustine was born in Thagaste (modern Algeria) and became bishop of Hippo Regius (modern Annaba, Algeria). His Latin represents the educated register of Roman Africa—the very region where African Romance would have evolved.
