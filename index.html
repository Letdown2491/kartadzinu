Overview

Kartadzinu is a constructed Romance language representing the fictional evolution of Latin in North Africa. Named for Carthage, the great city refounded by Rome, Kartadzinu developed with significant influence from the Berber (Amazigh) languages of the region. Unlike the Romance languages of Europe, it preserves features lost elsewhere while developing unique innovations.

Key Features

  • Aspect-prominent verbal system — Distinguishes perfective (completed) from imperfective (ongoing) actions
  • Construct state — Possessive suffixes attach directly to nouns for inalienable possession
  • VSO word order — In narrative and formal registers
  • Berber influence — Borrowed vocabulary and grammatical features
  • Cleft constructions — For focus and emphasis
  • Inclusive/exclusive "we" — nós (exclusive) vs nósin (inclusive)
Bona vja! Kom stas?
Hello! How are you?

Kartadzinu has seven vowels, preserving a distinction from Latin lost in most Romance languages. Like Sardinian (and probably African Romance), it distinguishes open and closed e/o:

Vowel origins: Open e/o come from Latin short Ĕ/Ŏ. Closed é/ó come from Latin long Ē/Ō or short Ĭ/Ŭ.

Tip for English speakers: English "bay" and "go" end with glides (/eɪ/ and /oʊ/). Kartadzinu é and ó are pure vowels—hold the mouth position steady without moving to another sound.

Stress

Stress is predictable:

  • Penultimate (second-to-last) syllable in most words: KA-sa, a-MI-ku
  • Antepenultimate (third-to-last) in certain patterns: DÓ-mi-nu, FE-mi-na

The accents é and ó mark vowel quality, not stress.

Consonants

No Palatalization: Unlike Italian or Spanish, Kartadzinu preserves Latin /k/ and /g/ before all vowels — a feature shared with Sardinian and attested in African Latin inscriptions.

Letters Sound Example
k, c /k/ always kelu "sky", kentu "hundred"
g /g/ always goia "joy", gente "people"
ts /ts/ (as in "cats") gratsia "grace", aktsione "action"
dj /dʒ/ (as in "judge") djustu "just"
nj /ɲ/ (as in "canyon") sinjare "to sign"
kw, qu /kw/ (as in "queen") kwatru, quando "four, when"
lj /ʎ/ (as in "million") palju "cloak"
r /r/ (trilled, as in Spanish) rosa "rose"
rr /rː/ (longer trill) ferru "iron", terra "earth"

Double consonants are held longer: terra /ter-ra/, anno /an-no/, bello /bel-lo/.

Aspect System

Kartadzinu is aspect-prominent, distinguishing:

  • Perfective — Completed action: Kantai una kantsone. "I sang a song."
  • Imperfective — Ongoing/habitual: Kantava kada dia. "I used to sing every day."

Aspect Markers

  • ja — already, marks completed state: Ja arrivau. "He has already arrived."
  • aset — still, marks ongoing state: Aset kerkamu. "We're still seeking."
  • tra- — completive preverb: tra-legere "to read through"

Basic Word Order

  • SVO — Normal/informal: Maria kanta una kantsone.
  • VSO — Narrative/formal: Kantau Maria una kantsone.

Questions

  • Intonation: Tu venes? "You're coming?"
  • Optional particle wa: Wa venes kon nos? "Are you coming with us?"
  • Question words: ke (what), ki (who), onde (where), kandu (when), kom (how), porke (why)

Cleft Constructions (Focus)

Use E...ke pattern for emphasis:

E Maria ke kantau.
"It was Maria who sang." (agent focus)
E la kantsone ke Maria kantau.
"It was the song that Maria sang." (patient focus)
