## How to Use This Dictionary

### Entry Format

Each entry follows this structure:

**headword** /pronunciation/ *part of speech, grammatical info*
1. definition
   - *Example sentence.* "Translation."

### Vowel Marking

Kartadzinu has seven vowels. The acute accent marks closed vowels:
- **e** /ɛ/ = open (from Latin short Ĕ)
- **é** /e/ = closed (from Latin long Ē or short Ĭ)
- **o** /ɔ/ = open (from Latin short Ŏ)
- **ó** /o/ = closed (from Latin long Ō or short Ŭ)

Examples: *bene* /bɛne/ "well" vs *kéna* /kena/ "dinner"; *bonu* /bɔnu/ "good" vs *sólu* /solu/ "alone"

### Gender and Plurals

Nouns are listed with gender (m./f.) and irregular plurals noted.
- Regular m. nouns: -u → -us (*libru* → *librus*)
- Regular f. nouns: -a → -as (*kasa* → *kasas*)
- Consonant-final: add -us/-as or -es (*patre* → *patres*)

### Verb Classes

Verbs are listed with their conjugation class:
- **Class I** (-are): *kantare* "to sing"
- **Class II** (-ere): *vedere* "to see"
- **Class III** (-ire): *dormire* "to sleep"

# Appendix C: Grammar Notes for Dictionary Users

## The Construct State
When a possessive suffix is attached directly to a noun, this is called the **construct state**. It's used for inalienable possession (body parts, family, abstract qualities):
- *kor-meu* "my heart" (heart-my)
- *patr-meu* "my father" (father-my)
- *amor-ela* "his/her love" (love-his/her)

## Contractions
In colloquial speech, prepositions contract with articles:
- *de lu* → *du* | *de la* → *dela*
- *a lu* → *al* | *a la* → *ala*
- *en lu* → *nu* | *en la* → *nela*

## Aspect Markers
- **ja** "already" — marks completed/achieved state
- **aset** "still" — marks ongoing state
- **tra-** (preverb) — marks action through to completion

## Inclusive vs. Exclusive "We"
- *nos* — we (excluding the listener)
- *nosin* — we (including the listener)
