Translation Library

Bilingual texts showcasing Kartadzinu's expressive range. Each translation demonstrates different features of the language, from mystical poetry to dramatic dialogue.

Imnu de la Perla

The Hymn of the Pearl | 2nd-3rd century CE

A Gnostic allegory from the Acts of Thomas. A prince journeys to Egypt to retrieve a pearl guarded by a serpent—the soul's descent into matter and awakening to its divine origin.

Gnostic Syriac Construct State VSO Narrative

Lu Tronu, Mente Perfeta

The Thunder, Perfect Mind | Nag Hammadi Library

A feminine divine voice speaks in paradoxes: "I am the honored one and the scorned one. I am the whore and the holy one." Transcending all dualities.

Gnostic Coptic Participles Cleft Constructions

Odas de Salomone

Odes of Solomon | c. 80-125 CE

Early Christian mystical hymns rich with imagery of light, water, and divine love. Among the earliest Christian devotional poetry.

Early Christian Syriac Water Imagery Telicity Markers

Konfesiones de Augustinu

Sero Te Amavi | Augustine of Hippo, c. 397-400 AD

"Tard te amai, belesa tan antika e tan noba!" The famous "Late Have I Loved You" passage from North Africa's greatest Latin writer, with its sensory imagery of divine encounter.

Patristic North African Betacism Construct State

Djariu de Perpetua

The Passion of Perpetua | Carthage, 203 AD

"So una Kristjana." A young Carthaginian mother's prison diary—visions of ladders and dragons, defiance before judges, and triumph over death. One of the earliest texts by a Christian woman.

Martyrdom North African First-Person Narrative Betacism

Apologetiku de Tertullianu

Apologeticum L | Tertullian of Carthage, 197 AD

"Semente es lu sangu de lus Kristjanus." The famous "blood of Christians is seed" passage—persecution spreads the faith. From the first great Latin Christian writer.

Apologetics North African Rhetorical Prose Passive Voice

Kupidu e Psike

The Golden Ass | Apuleius of Madauros, c. 170 AD

"Bidai la plu mite e la plu dulse de totas las kreaturas." The famous revelation scene where Psyche discovers her husband is the god of love—from North Africa's greatest Latin novelist.

Novel North African Descriptive Prose VSO Narrative

Psalmu 137

Super Flumina Babylonis | Vulgate, c. 405 AD

"Komodu kantaremu lu kantiku du Dominu en terra aljena?" The lament of the exiles in Babylon—a meditation on memory, homeland, and the silencing of songs.

Psalms Latin/Hebrew Imperfective Past Subjunctive

Romeo e Guljeta

Romeo and Juliet: The Balcony Scene | Shakespeare, c. 1595

"O Romeo, Romeo! porke ses tu Romeo?" The famous balcony scene showcasing Kartadzinu's capacity for poetic register and light/dark imagery.

Drama English Poetic Register Subjunctive

About the Translations

Each translation is presented in bilingual format with the English original alongside the Kartadzinu version. Translations include:

  • Introduction — Context about the source text and which Kartadzinu features it showcases
  • Bilingual Text — Side-by-side English and Kartadzinu
  • Translation Notes — Detailed explanation of grammatical features and vocabulary
