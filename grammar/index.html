Preface

This grammar presents Kartadzinu, the language of Carthage — a Romance language representing the fictional evolution of Latin in North Africa. Unlike the Romance languages of Europe (Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese, Romanian), Kartadzinu developed in isolation with significant influence from the Berber (Amazigh) languages of the region.

This guide is designed for learners. Each chapter builds on the previous, with clear explanations, abundant examples, and exercises to reinforce learning. By the end, you will be able to read, write, and construct original sentences in Kartadzinu.

Quick Start

New to Kartadzinu? Here's a suggested learning path:

  1. Start with sounds — Part I teaches you to pronounce and read Kartadzinu correctly
  2. Learn the basics — Parts II-IV cover nouns, pronouns, and adjectives
  3. Master the verbs — Part V is the heart of the language (take your time here!)
  4. Build sentences — Part VI shows how to put it all together
  5. Explore special features — Part VII covers what makes Kartadzinu unique
  6. Reference as needed — Appendices provide tables and answers
