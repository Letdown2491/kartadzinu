Appendix E: Glossary of Grammatical Terms

Aspect
A grammatical category describing whether an action is complete (perfective) or ongoing (imperfective).
Construct State
A special noun form used when directly linked to another noun, typically for inalienable possession.
Focus
The information in a sentence that is new or emphasized. Kartadzinu marks this with cleft constructions (E... ke).
Imperfective
Aspect viewing action as ongoing, habitual, or unbounded.
Inclusive/Exclusive
Distinction in first person plural: inclusive (nos) includes the listener; exclusive (nosin) excludes them.
Perfective
Aspect viewing action as complete, bounded, or a single whole.
Telicity
The property of an action having or lacking a natural endpoint. Marked by ja, aset, tra-.
VSO
Verb-Subject-Object word order, used in Kartadzinu for narrative, religious, and formal registers.
