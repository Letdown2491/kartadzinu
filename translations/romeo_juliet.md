# ROMEO AND JULIET: THE BALCONY SCENE / ROMEO E GULJETA: LA STSENA DE BALKONU
## Bilingual Edition: English - Kartadzinu

*Act II, Scene 2 from William Shakespeare's tragedy (c. 1595)*

---

## Introduction

**The Balcony Scene** from Romeo and Juliet is one of the most famous passages in all of Western literature. Romeo, having scaled the Capulet orchard wall, sees Juliet appear at her window and speaks of his love in rich metaphorical language comparing her to the sun, the stars, and an angel.

This text showcases Kartadzinu's capacity for:

- **Poetic register**: Elevated vocabulary and VSO constructions
- **Light/dark imagery**: Using Kartadzinu's semantic field where *luts* means both light and understanding
- **Vocatives and imperatives**: Direct address forms
- **Subjunctive mood**: For wishes and hypotheticals
- **Rhetorical questions**: Question formation
- **Comparative and superlative**: Poetic comparisons

The English text follows the standard Shakespeare edition.

## I. ROMEO'S SOLILOQUY / LU SOLILOKVIU DE ROMEO

**English:**
But, soft! what light through yonder window breaks?
It is the east, and Juliet is the sun.
Arise, fair sun, and kill the envious moon,
Who is already sick and pale with grief,
That thou her maid art far more fair than she:
Be not her maid, since she is envious;
Her vestal livery is but sick and green
And none but fools do wear it; cast it off.

It is my lady, O, it is my love!
O, that she knew she were!
She speaks yet she says nothing: what of that?
Her eye discourses; I will answer it.
I am too bold, 'tis not to me she speaks:
Two of the fairest stars in all the heaven,
Having some business, do entreat her eyes
To twinkle in their spheres till they return.

**Kartadzinu:**
Ma, kalmu! ke luts tra kela fenestra rompe?
E lu oriente, e Guljeta e lu sól.
Alzate, sól belu, e oktsidite la luna invitsjosa,
Ke ja e malata e palida kon dolóru,
Ke tu serva-ela ses lon plu bela ke ela:
Non siets serva-ela, porke ela e invitsjosa;
Vestimentu-ela vestale e solamente malatu e verde
E nulu ekseptu stultus lu portan; egektate-lu.

E domina-mea, O, e amor-meu!
O, ke ela sapesse ke ela fuvit!
Ela parla ma non dise nula: ke de istu?
Okulu-ela diskursa; eu responderu a-elu.
So trop audatsu, non e a me ke ela parla:
Dua de lus stellas plu belas en totalmente lu kelu,
Avivendu algun negotsju, suplikan okulus-ela
Por brilyare en sferas-sus fina ke retornan.

## II. JULIET APPEARS / GULJETA APARE

**English:**
What if her eyes were there, they in her head?
The brightness of her cheek would shame those stars,
As daylight doth a lamp; her eyes in heaven
Would through the airy region stream so bright
That birds would sing and think it were not night.

See, how she leans her cheek upon her hand!
O, that I were a glove upon that hand,
That I might touch that cheek!

**Kartadzinu:**
Ke se okulus-ela fuvessen ili, elus en kapu-ela?
La brillantsia de gena-ela vergongerit kelas stellas,
Kom luts-dia faze a una lukerna; okulus-ela en kelu
Fluessen tra la redjone aerea tan brillantemente
Ke lus aves kanteressen e penseressen ke non fuvit note.

Regardate, kom ela inklina gena-ela su manu-ela!
O, ke eu fuvesse un gwantu su kela manu,
Por ke podesse tatsare kela gena!

## III. JULIET SPEAKS / GULJETA PARLA

**English:**
JULIET:
Ay me!

ROMEO:
She speaks:
O, speak again, bright angel! for thou art
As glorious to this night, being o'er my head
As is a winged messenger of heaven
Unto the white-upturned wondering eyes
Of mortals that fall back to gaze on him
When he bestrides the lazy-pacing clouds
And sails upon the bosom of the air.

JULIET:
O Romeo, Romeo! wherefore art thou Romeo?
Deny thy father and refuse thy name;
Or, if thou wilt not, be but sworn my love,
And I'll no longer be a Capulet.

**Kartadzinu:**
GULJETA:
Ai me!

ROMEO:
Ela parla:
O, parlate ja-mais, anjelu brillante! porke tu ses
Tan glórjósa a ista note, esendu supra kapu-meu
Kom e un mesageru alatu de kelu
A lus okulus blankus-tornatus meravilatus
De mortalus ke kadan retru por regardare-lu
Kandu elu kavalka lus nubes lentamente-passantes
E naviga su lu sinu de lu aere.

GULJETA:
O Romeo, Romeo! porke ses tu Romeo?
Nega patre-te e refusa nóme-te;
O, se tu non vules, siets solamente guratu amor-meu,
E eu non seru plu una Kapulet.

## IV. WHAT'S IN A NAME / KE E EN UN NOME

**English:**
JULIET:
'Tis but thy name that is my enemy;
Thou art thyself, though not a Montague.
What's Montague? it is nor hand, nor foot,
Nor arm, nor face, nor any other part
Belonging to a man. O, be some other name!
What's in a name? that which we call a rose
By any other name would smell as sweet;
So Romeo would, were he not Romeo call'd,
Retain that dear perfection which he owes
Without that title. Romeo, doff thy name,
And for that name which is no part of thee
Take all myself.

ROMEO:
I take thee at thy word:
Call me but love, and I'll be new baptized;
Henceforth I never will be Romeo.

**Kartadzinu:**
GULJETA:
E solamente nóme-te ke e inimigu-meu;
Tu ses tu-mesu, ankam non un Montagu.
Ke e Montagu? non e manu, ni pe,
Ni bratsu, ni fatsa, ni nula altra parte
Apartenente a un omu. O, siets algun altru nóme!
Ke e en un nóme? kelu ke nós klamamu una rosa
Per alkun altru nóme oleret tan dultsemente;
Alsi Romeo, se elu non fuvesse klamatu Romeo,
Retenerit kela kara perfetsione ke elu deve
Sen kelu titulu. Romeo, egektate nóme-te,
E por kelu nóme ke non e parte de te
Prendite totalmente me-mesu.

ROMEO:
Prendu te a parabula-tea:
Klamate me solamente amor, e seru novu batizatu;
De ora avante numka seru Romeo.

## V. THE VOW / LU GURAMENTU

**English:**
JULIET:
How camest thou hither, tell me, and wherefore?
The orchard walls are high and hard to climb,
And the place death, considering who thou art,
If any of my kinsmen find thee here.

ROMEO:
With love's light wings did I o'er-perch these walls;
For stony limits cannot hold love out,
And what love can do that dares love attempt;
Therefore thy kinsmen are no let to me.

JULIET:
If they do see thee, they will murder thee.

ROMEO:
Alack, there lies more peril in thine eye
Than twenty of their swords: look thou but sweet,
And I am proof against their enmity.

**Kartadzinu:**
GULJETA:
Kom vinisti tu iki, dise-me, e porke?
Lus muros de lu pomarju son altus e difitsilus por astsendere,
E lu loku e morte, konsiderantu ki tu ses,
Se alkunu de parentus-meus trova te iki.

ROMEO:
Kon alas leveres de amor superai istus muros;
Porke limitus petreus non podan tenere amor ekstra,
E ke amor pode fare, istu auda amor tentare;
Pore parentus-teus non son impedimentu a me.

GULJETA:
Se elus vidan te, elus oktsideranu te.

ROMEO:
Ai, gatse plu perikulu en okulu-te
Ke vinti de spadus-sus: regardate solamente dultsemente,
E so protegitu kontra inimikitsa-sus.

---

## VI. PARTING / LA SEPARATSIONE

**English:**
JULIET:
Good night, good night! parting is such sweet sorrow,
That I shall say good night till it be morrow.

ROMEO:
Sleep dwell upon thine eyes, peace in thy breast!
Would I were sleep and peace, so sweet to rest!

**Kartadzinu:**
GULJETA:
Bona note, bona note! la separatsione e tan dulke dolóru,
Ke diseru bona note fina ke sie matinu.

ROMEO:
Ke somnu abite en okulus-teus, pats en petu-te!
Ke eu fuvesse somnu e pats, tan dulkes por repozare!

## Translation Notes

### Features Showcased

**1. Poetic VSO Word Order**
Elevated register throughout:
- *Alzate, sól belu* "Arise, fair sun"
- *Fluessen tra la redjone* "Would stream through the region"
- *Gatse plu perikulu* "Lies more peril"

**2. Light/Dark Semantic Field**
Kartadzinu's *luts* carries metaphorical weight:
- *ke luts tra kela fenestra rompe* "what light through yonder window breaks"
- *anjelu brillante* "bright angel"
- *luts-dia* "daylight" (compound)

**3. Subjunctive for Wishes**
- *ke ela sapesse* "that she knew" (imperfect subjunctive)
- *ke eu fuvesse un gwantu* "that I were a glove"
- *ke somnu abite* "may sleep dwell"

**4. Construct State for Intimacy**
- *amor-meu* "my love"
- *domina-mea* "my lady"
- *okulu-ela* "her eye"
- *gena-ela* "her cheek"

**5. Rhetorical Questions**
- *Ke e en un nóme?* "What's in a name?"
- *Ke e Montagu?* "What's Montague?"
- *porke ses tu Romeo?* "wherefore art thou Romeo?"

**6. Imperative Forms**
- *Alzate* "Arise" (2SG formal/poetic)
- *Regardate* "See/Look"
- *Nega...refusa* "Deny...refuse"
