The Hymn of the Pearl / Imnu de la Perla

Bilingual Edition: English - Kartadzinu

A Gnostic text from the Acts of Thomas

Introduction

The Hymn of the Pearl (also known as the Hymn of the Soul) is a Gnostic poem preserved within the apocryphal Acts of Thomas, likely composed in Syriac in the 2nd-3rd century CE. It tells an allegorical story of a prince sent from the East to Egypt to retrieve a pearl guarded by a serpent—widely interpreted as the soul's descent into the material world and its awakening to its divine origin.

This text showcases several distinctive Kartadzinu features:

  • Construct state: For inalienable possession and titles (rejn-patr-meu "my father's kingdom", patr-matri "father-mother")
  • VSO word order: In narrative passages for dramatic effect (Alora partiai de Oriente)
  • Telicity markers: ja marking completed states and achievements
  • Cleft constructions: For emphasis (E ela ke... "It is she who...")
  • Participles as predicates: Describing ongoing states (nutriente, kondukentes)
  • Semantic shifts: via as spiritual path, sapere for direct knowing

The English text is adapted from scholarly translations of the Syriac original.

I. The Departure / Lu Partimentu

English

When I was still a child,
dwelling in my father's kingdom,
resting in the riches and luxury
of my father's mother who nurtured me,

they prepared provisions for my journey —
a great bundle but light,
that I could carry alone.

They sent gold from Bet-Ellaye, our highland,
and silver from the treasurers of Gazak,
place of great riches.

They armed me with adamant
that can break iron.

They pulled off from me my glory-robe,
and my purple cloak,
sewn according to my greatness.

Kartadzinu

Kandu fui eu ankora infantu,
abitandu en rejn-patr-meu,
repozandu en rikisi e luxsu
de matri-patr-meu nutriente,

prepararu-me provizjoni por lu viaggu —
paketu grandu ma levere,
ke podesse portare eu solu.

Oru mandaru de Bet-Ellaje, terr-altur-nostru,
e argentu de lu trezoreri-Gazzak,
loku de rikisi magnae.

Armaru-me kon adamantu
ke pode romppere ferru.

Tiraveru de me mea tóga-glória,
e meu paliu purpureu,
kesitus sekundu mea grandesa.

II. The Pact / Lu Paktu

English

They made a pact with me
and wrote it in my heart, that I might not forget:

"If you descend to Egypt
and bring back the One Pearl,
that which rests in the midst of the Roaring Sea,
surrounded by the winging Serpent,

then you shall put on again your glory-robe
and your purple cloak over it,
and you shall become a hero with your second brother,
and you shall be a deputy in our Father's kingdom."

Kartadzinu

Fazaru paktu kon me
e skribiveru-lu en kor-meu, median non obliviesse:

"Se tu deshendes a Egitu
e portas la Perla Unu,
kela ke repoza en mesu de Mar-Raukantu,
tsirkum de Serpente ruflante,

alora vestira-te ja tu tóga-glória
e teu paliu purpureu supra-ela,
e devenirats herovu kon fratru-te sekundu,
e seirats deputatu en rejn-Patr-nostru."

III. The Journey / Lu Viaggu

English

Then I departed from the East,
descending a difficult and dangerous journey,
with two guides leading.

I did not know the way,
for I was still young to travel.

When we crossed the borders of Maishan,
meeting place of merchants of the East,
and we arrived in the lands of Babylon —

we entered Sarbug, city of Bet-Ellaye.

Kartadzinu

Alora partiai de Oriente,
deshendendu viaggu difitsilu e perikulozu,
kon doi guidas kondukentes.

Non koneseva eu la via,
porke fui ankora djovene por viagare.

Kandu traversaimu frontjeri de Majshan,
loku de rendevu de merkatoris de Oriente,
e arrivaimu a terras de Babilonia —

entraimus en Sarbug, kivitate de Bet-Ellaje.

IV. Among the Egyptians / Entre lus Egitus

English

Then we descended to Egypt.
My companions separated from me.

I went directly toward the Serpent,
dwelling near his habitation,
waiting while he slept and rested,
so that I could take the Pearl from him.

But because I was alone,
a stranger to my companions of habitation,
they saw me, a man of my race,
young and beautiful, son of kings,
one of our anointed.

We became intimate and companions,
that we might warn one another against the Egyptians
and against commerce with the impure.

But I dressed in their garments,
lest they suspect I was a stranger,
come from outside to take the Pearl,
and arouse the Serpent against me.

Kartadzinu

Alora deshendimu a Egitu.
Separaveru-se de me meus kompanjunes.

Iaimu direktamente vers Serpente,
abitandu pres habitatsione-ela,
aspetandu medan kelu dormiesse e repozesse,
por ke podesse prendere la Perla de-elu.

Ma porke fui eu solu,
ekstraneru a meus kompanjunus de abitatsione,
videveru-me unu omu de rasa-mea,
djovene e belu, filu de reges,
unu de untsutu-nostru.

Devinimu intimi e kompanjunus,
ke avertisse lu unu lu altru kontra lus Egitus
e kontra lu kommerse kon lus impurus.

Ma vestiaime kon vestitus-sus,
medan non suspetessen ke fui ekstraneru,
venentu de fora por portare la Perla,
e ekstsissen Serpente kontra me.

V. The Forgetting / Lu Oblividu

English

But somehow — I know not how —
they learned that I was not their kinsman.

I dealt with them
and tasted their food.

I forgot that I was a son of kings,
and I served their king.

I forgot the Pearl
for which my parents had sent me.

And through the heaviness of their foods,
I fell into deep sleep.

Kartadzinu

Ma algun manera — non saju kom —
sapiveru kelu ke non fui sus parente.

Kommersiaime kon-sus
e gustavaimu tsibu-sus.

Obliviaime ke fui filu de reges,
e serviaime rege-sus.

Obliviaime la Perla
por kela kualit mandaveru-me meus parentes.

E tra lu pesu de tsibus-sus,
kajaime en somnu profundu.

VI. The Letter from Father-Mother / La Lettera de Patr-Matri

English

But all this that happened to me,
my father-mother knew and grieved for me.

Then a proclamation was published in our kingdom,
that all should not come to our harbor.

And they wrote me a letter
from the King of kings, my father,
and from my mother-Queen and from my second brother:

"From your father, King of kings,
and from your mother, Ruler of the East,
and from your brother, the second in authority,
to our son in Egypt, greetings!

Awaken and arise from your sleep!
Hear the words of our letter!

Remember that you are a son of kings!
See the servitude in which you are serving!

Remember the Pearl,
for which we sent you to Egypt!

Remember your glory-robe
and your splendid cloak,
that you may wear them and adorn yourself with them
and your name may be read in the book of heroes,
and you may become a deputy with your brother in our kingdom!"

Kartadzinu

Ma totalmente istu ke akontekaivit a me,
sapiveru-lu patr-matri-meu e doliveru por me.

Alora publikavit proklamatsione en rejn-nostru,
ke totus non venessen a portu-nostru.

E skribiveru a me lettera
de Rege de reges, patr-meu,
e de Rejina-matr-mea e de fratru-meu sekundu:

"De patre-te, Rege de reges,
e de matri-tea, Dominatritsa de Oriente,
e de fratru-te, kelu sekundu en autoritatu,
a filu-nostru en Egitu, salutatsione!

Desperta-te e levana-te de somnu-te!
Ode palavras de lettera-nostra!

Rekorda ke ses filu de reges!
Vide kelu servitudine onde stas servante!

Memora la Perla,
por kela mandaimu-te a Egitu!

Rekorda tóga-tea de glória
e paliu-te splendidu,
por ke portes-lus e ornes-te kon-lus
e nóme-te sie letu en libru de heróvis,
e devenes deputatu kon fratru-te en rejn-nostru!"

VII. The Awakening / Lu Despertainamentu

English

Like a royal seal and command,
the letter came to me.

It flew in the form of an eagle,
king of all birds,
and it flew until it settled near me
and became entirely words.

At its voice and its sound,
I awoke and arose from my sleep.

I took it and kissed it,
I broke its seal and read.

And according to what was written in my heart,
so it was written in my letter.

I remembered that I was a son of kings
and my free soul desired its own.

I remembered the Pearl
for which they had sent me to Egypt.

And I began to enchant the terrible Serpent
and to subdue him by the name of my Father
and the name of my second brother
and the name of our Mother, Queen of the East.

Kartadzinu

Kom sigillatu rejalu e mandamentu,
vinistu-me la lettera.

Volavit en forma de agila,
rege de totus lus aves,
e fluvit fina ke asentistu pres me
e devinistu totalmente palavras.

A vots-ela e soinu-ela,
destavaimu e levanaimu de somnu-meu.

Prenai-la e baziai-la,
romppai sigillatu-ela e legai.

E sekundu ke skribitu fut en kor-meu,
alsi fut skritu en lettera-mea.

Rekordai ke fui filu de reges
e anima-mea libera desiravit sus proprius.

Rekordai la Perla
por kela mandaveru-me a Egitu.

E komentsai enkantare Serpente teribile
e sutszugare-lu per nóme de Patr-meu
e nóme de fratru-meu sekundu
e nóme de Matri-nostra, Rejina de Oriente.

VIII. The Pearl / La Perla

English

I seized the Pearl
and turned toward the house of my Father.

Their filthy and impure garments
I stripped off and left in their lands,
and I directed my way
toward the light of our homeland, the East.

And my letter, my awakener,
I found before me on the way.

And as it had awakened me by its voice,
so it guided me by its light.

It was written on royal silk,
gleaming before me,
encouraging me by its voice
and drawing me by its love.

Kartadzinu

Arpai la Perla
e turnai vers domo de Patr-meu.

Vestitus-sus sukidus e impurus
despulai e lasai en terras-sus,
e direktaime vja-mea
vers luts de patria-nostra, Oriente.

E lettera-mea, despertainadore-meu,
trovai ante me su la via.

E kom despertainau-me per vots-ela,
alsi gwidavit-me per luts-ela.

Skritu fut su seda rejala,
brillandu ante me,
inkuragendu-me per vots-ela
e trahevut-me per amor-ela.

IX. The Glory-Robe / La Tóga-Glória

English

I rushed forward and crossed Sarbug.
I left Babylon to my left,
and I arrived at Maishan the Great,
harbor of the sea-merchants.

And my glory-robe,
which they had stripped from me,
and my cloak which they had carried over it,
my father-mother sent them
from the heights of Hyrcania,
by the hand of their treasurers,
in whom they trusted for their faithfulness.

I did not remember its form,
for I had left it in my childhood in my Father's house.

But suddenly, when I saw it,
the image gleamed as in a mirror:
I saw in it my entire self,
and I recognized myself in it,
though we were two in separation
but already one in a single form.

Kartadzinu

Rusai avante e traversai Sarbug.
Lasai Babilonia a sinistra-mea,
e arrivai a Majshan la Granda,
portu de merkatoris-mar.

E mea tóga-glória,
kela tiraverame,
e paliu-meu kelu portaverame supra,
mandaveru-lus patr-matri-meu
de alturas de Hyrcania,
per manu de trezoreris-sus,
en ke kofidevut-sus por fidelitatu-sus.

Non rekordava eu forma-ela,
porke lasaverame-la en infantsa-mea en domo de Patr-meu.

Ma subitu, kandu vidai-la,
image brillavit kom en spekulu:
vidai en ela totalmente me-mesu,
e rekonesai me en ela,
medan nos fussemus doi en separatsione
ma ja unu en forma sola.

X. The Union / La Unione

English

And the treasurers, carrying it,
I saw them also in two forms
for one was their action
but the royal sign was one in both.

Riches I saw on the robe,
preparing itself to gleam,
and love grew over all its movements.

It rushed toward me,
stretching from the hands of the bearers,
and my love urged me
that I might rush to it and receive it.

And I stretched out my hands and took it,
and I adorned myself with its beauty.

I clothed myself entirely in my purple and gleaming cloak.

I put it on, and I rose up to the giver-kingdom
that had carried it to me in my childhood.

Kartadzinu

E trezoreris, portantes,
vidai-lus alsi en doi formas
porke unu fue aktsione-sus
ma signalu rejalu unu en ambos.

Rikisi observai su toga,
preparante-se por brillare,
e amor kreskivit su totalmente movimentus.

Rusavit vers me,
tendendu-se de manus de portatoris,
e amor-meu inkitau-me
por ke rusesse a ela e retsebesse-la.

E tendai manus-meas e prenai-la,
e ornaime kon-la belisa-ela.

Vestiai totalmente me en paliu-meu purpureu e brillante.

Vestiai-lu, e alzaime fina rejn-donatoris
ke portaverame-lu en infantsa.

XI. The Complete Remembering / Lu Rekordamentu Kompletu

English

And I already remembered all its splendor,
how it was nurtured in my Father's house.

My soul already recalled
its ranks and its greatness.

So it was woven when it was in my Father's house:
a marvelous movement was in all its form.

In colors of gold and beryl,
rubies and agates,
sardonyxes and multicolored pearls —

it was inclined for highest service.

And according to the Wisdom of the heights
all its seams conversed with me,
and I loved it as a companion
woven from my very self.

Kartadzinu

E ja rekordava totalmente su splendoru,
kom nutritu fut en domo de Patr-meu.

Alma-mea ja memoristu
gradevis-ela e grandesa-ela.

Alsi fut teksitu kandu fut en domo de Patr-meu:
movimentu mirabile fut en totalmente forma-ela.

En kolorus de oru e berilu,
rubji e agati,
sardoniki e perlas multikoloris —

inklinatu fut por servitsiu altisime.

E sekundu Sapientsia de alturas
totalmente suturae-ela konversaveru kon me,
e amaime-la kom kompanjune
teksuta de mesu-mesu.

XII. The Return / Lu Retornu

English

And I saw that a trembling
was preparing itself throughout all of it,
the movement of knowledge.

I saw that it was already preparing itself to speak.

I heard the murmur of its sound:

"I am of him most active among the servants,
for whom my Father made me.

And I knew that my stature grew
according to my labors."

And with royal movements
it poured itself toward me entirely,
and from the hands of the bearers
it hastened that I might already receive it.

And my love urged me also
that I might rush toward it and receive it.

And I stretched it out and took it,
and I adorned myself with the beauty of its colors.

I clothed myself entirely
in my royal and splendid robe.

Kartadzinu

E vidai ke tremolatsione
preparastu-se tra totalmente-ela,
movimentu de konjitsione.

Vidai ke ja parastu-se por parlare.

Odai murmuratione de sonu-ela:

"Eu so de kelu plu aktive inter lus servis,
por kelu fatserame Patre meu.

E sapai ke kreskistu statura-mea
sekundu laboris-meu."

E kon movimentus rejalis
versastu-se vers me totalmente,
e de manus de portatoris
pretsipavit-se ke ja retsebesse-la.

E amor-meu inkitau-me alsi
por ke rusesse vers-ela e retsebesse-la.

E tendai-la e prenai-la,
e ornaime kon-la belisa de kolorus-ela.

Vestiai me totalmente
en toga-mea rejala e splendida.

XIII. The Ascension / La Asensione

English

Clothed in it,
I rose up to the harbor-peace
of bowing and worship.

I bowed my head and worshiped
the splendor of the Father who had sent it to me,
whose commandments I had fulfilled
and I also had fulfilled his commission.

I mingled myself with the distinguished ones,
the first ones before the royal harbor.

And he received me into his house,
and all his servants venerated.

And I promised the Father
that soon I would go to the harbor
of the King of kings
and carrying my Pearl,
I would appear before him.

Kartadzinu

Vestitu en-ela,
alzaime fina portu-pats
de inklinatsione e adoratsione.

Inklinai kapu-meu e adorai
splendoru de Patre ke mandau-la a me,
ke perfektu fue mandamentus-ela
e eu alsi perfektu fue komisione-ela.

Meskai me kon lus distinktus,
lus primu ante portu rejala.

E retsebau-me en domo-ela,
e totalmente servis-ela veneraveru.

E promittau Patre
ke presto iressu fina portu
de Rege de reges
e portandu Perla-mea,
aparessu ante-elu.

Translation Notes

Features Showcased

1. Construct State

Used extensively for inalienable possession and compound concepts:

  • rejn-patr-meu "my father's kingdom" (kingdom-father-my)
  • patr-matri-meu "my father-mother" (divine parental unity)
  • tóga-glória "glory-robe" (fixed compound)
  • terr-altur-nostru "our highland" (land-heights-our)

2. VSO Word Order

Narrative/dramatic passages use VSO for effect:

  • Alora partiai de Oriente "Then departed-I from the East"
  • Alora deshendimu a Egitu "Then descended-we to Egypt"
  • Vinistu-me la lettera "Came-to-me the letter"

3. Telicity Markers

  • ja marks achieved states: ja unu en forma sola "already one in a single form"
  • Perfective aspect for bounded narrative events

4. Participles as Predicates

  • nutriente "nurturing" (describing the grandmother)
  • kondukentes "leading" (describing the guides)
  • portantes "carrying" (describing the treasurers)
