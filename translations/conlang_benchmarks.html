Bilingual Edition: English - Kartadzinu

Standard texts used by constructed language creators to showcase their languages

Introduction

These texts represent the traditional benchmarks used by conlangers (constructed language creators) to demonstrate their language's capabilities. Each text tests different aspects of grammar and vocabulary:

  • The North Wind and the Sun: The IPA standard text for phonetic comparison; tests narrative, comparison, and causative constructions
  • Schleicher's Fable: The Proto-Indo-European benchmark; tests basic narrative and reported speech
  • The Hobbit Opening: A tribute to Tolkien, father of modern conlanging; tests descriptive prose and existential constructions
  • The Tower of Babel: Genesis 11:1-9; tests narrative and divine speech

These translations showcase Kartadzinu's features:

  • Aspect system: Perfective for completed narrative events, imperfective for states
  • VSO word order: In narrative contexts
  • Comparative constructions: plu...ke for comparisons
  • Causative constructions: fare + infinitive

I. The North Wind and the Sun / Lu Ventu Nordu e lu Sól

The standard IPA illustration text, from Aesop's Fables

English

The North Wind and the Sun were disputing which was the stronger, when a traveler came along wrapped in a warm cloak.

They agreed that the one who first succeeded in making the traveler take his cloak off should be considered stronger than the other.

Then the North Wind blew as hard as he could, but the more he blew the more closely did the traveler fold his cloak around him; and at last the North Wind gave up the attempt.

Then the Sun shone out warmly, and immediately the traveler took off his cloak.

And so the North Wind was obliged to confess that the Sun was the stronger of the two.

Moral: Gentleness and kind persuasion win where force and bluster fail.

Kartadzinu

Lu Ventu Nordu e lu Sól disputavan kual fuvit lu plu forte, kandu vinit un viagante vestitu en un paliu kaldu.

Konkordaru ke kelu ke primu sutsedesse en fare ke lu viagante tirasse paliu-seu devesse essere konsideratu plu forte ke lu altru.

Alora sufflau lu Ventu Nordu kom plu forte ke podesse, ma kwantu plu sufflau, tantu plu stretamente plikau lu viagante paliu-seu attornu de-elu; e a la fine lu Ventu Nordu abandonau lu tentamentu.

Alora brillau lu Sól kaldamente, e immediatamente lu viagante tirau paliu-seu.

E alsi lu Ventu Nordu fue obligatu a konfessare ke lu Sól fuvit lu plu forte de lus doi.

Morale: Gentilesa e persuasione benigna vinkan onde fortsa e furia fallan.

II. Schleicher's Fable / La Fabula de Schleicher

The Proto-Indo-European benchmark text, composed by August Schleicher in 1868

English

A sheep that had no wool saw horses: one pulling a heavy wagon, one carrying a big load, and one carrying a man quickly.

The sheep said to the horses: "My heart pains me, seeing a man driving horses."

The horses said: "Listen, sheep! Our hearts pain us when we see this: a man, the master, makes the wool of the sheep into a warm garment for himself. And the sheep has no wool."

Having heard this, the sheep fled into the plain.

Kartadzinu

Una ovja ke non avivit lana vidau kavalus: unu tirantu un karru pesante, unu portantu un kargamentu grandu, e unu portantu un omu rapidamente.

La ovja disse a lus kavalus: "Kor-meu dole a me, videndu un omu kondukente kavalus."

Lus kavalus disseru: "Ode, ovja! Kor-nostrus dolan a nos kandu videmu istu: un omu, lu magistru, faze la lana de la ovja en vestimentu kaldu por se-mesu. E la ovja non a lana."

Avivendu oditu istu, la ovja fugiau a lu planu.

III. The Hobbit Opening / Lu Initsju de lu Hobbit

A tribute to J.R.R. Tolkien, the father of modern constructed languages

English

In a hole in the ground there lived a hobbit. Not a nasty, dirty, wet hole, filled with the ends of worms and an oozy smell, nor yet a dry, bare, sandy hole with nothing in it to sit down on or to eat: it was a hobbit-hole, and that means comfort.

Kartadzinu

En un foramine en la terra vivivit un hobbit. Non un foramine malu, sukidu, umidu, plenatu kon lus finus de vermis e un odore mukusu, ni ankora un foramine seku, nudu, arenosu sen nula en-elu por sedere o por mangare: fuvit un foramine-de-hobbit, e istu sinjifika komfortu.

IV. The Tower of Babel / Lu Tekstu de Babel

Genesis 11:1-9, the story of the Tower of Babel and the origin of languages

English

Now the whole earth had one language and the same words. And as people migrated from the east, they found a plain in the land of Shinar and settled there.

And they said to one another, "Come, let us make bricks, and burn them thoroughly." And they had brick for stone, and bitumen for mortar.

Then they said, "Come, let us build ourselves a city and a tower with its top in the heavens, and let us make a name for ourselves, lest we be dispersed over the face of the whole earth."

And the Lord came down to see the city and the tower, which the children of man had built. And the Lord said, "Behold, they are one people, and they have all one language, and this is only the beginning of what they will do. And nothing that they propose to do will now be impossible for them.

Come, let us go down and there confuse their language, so that they may not understand one another's speech."

So the Lord dispersed them from there over the face of all the earth, and they left off building the city. Therefore its name was called Babel, because there the Lord confused the language of all the earth. And from there the Lord dispersed them over the face of all the earth.

Kartadzinu

Ora la terra intera avivit una lingua e lus mesus palavras. E kom lus populus migraru de oriente, trovaru un planu en la terra de Shinar e establiru-se ili.

E disseru lu unu a lu altru, "Venite, fazemu lateris, e ardemu-lus totalmente." E aviveru lateri por petra, e bitume por mortaru.

Alora disseru, "Venite, edifikemu a nós-mésus una kivitate e una turre kon kapu-ela en lus kélus, e fazemu un nóme por nós-mésus, median non siemu dispersatus su la fatsa de la terra intera."

E lu Dóminu deshendau por videre la kivitate e la turre, ke lus filus de omu aviveru edifikatu. E lu Dóminu disse, "Regardate, elus son un populu, e an tótus una lingua, e istu e solamente lu initsju de ke elus faranu. E nula ke elus proponan fare serat ora impossibile por elus.

Venite, deshendemu e ili konfundemu lingua-sus, median non komprenden lu unu la parabula de lu altru."

Alsi lu Dóminu dispersau-lus de ili su la fatsa de la terra intera, e kessaru de edifikare la kivitate. Pore nóme-ela fue klamatu Babel, porke ili lu Dóminu konfundau la lingua de la terra intera. E de ili lu Dóminu dispersau-lus su la fatsa de la terra intera.

Translation Notes

Features Showcased

1. Comparative Constructions

  • plu forte ke "stronger than"
  • lu plu forte de lus doi "the stronger of the two"
  • kwantu plu...tantu plu "the more...the more"

2. Causative with FARE

  • fare ke lu viagante tirasse "to make the traveler take off"
  • faze la lana...en vestimentu "makes the wool into a garment"

3. VSO in Narrative

  • Alora sufflau lu Ventu Nordu "Then blew the North Wind"
  • Alora brillau lu Sól "Then shone the Sun"

4. Aspect Distinctions

  • Imperfective for states: disputavan "were disputing"
  • Perfective for bounded events: vidau "saw", disse "said"

5. Participles as Predicates

  • videndu un omu kondukente kavalus "seeing a man driving horses"
  • tirantu un karru "pulling a wagon"
  • avivendu oditu "having heard"

6. Construct State

  • kor-meu "my heart" (heart-my)
  • kor-nostrus "our hearts"
  • foramine-de-hobbit "hobbit-hole"
