The Thunder, Perfect Mind / Lu Tronu, Mente Perfeta

Bilingual Edition: English - Kartadzinu

A Gnostic revelation discourse from the Nag Hammadi Library

Introduction

The Thunder, Perfect Mind is one of the most striking texts from the Nag Hammadi collection, discovered in Egypt in 1945. A feminine divine voice speaks in paradoxical "I am" statements, transcending all dualities—honored and scorned, whore and holy one, wife and virgin, first and last.

This text is ideally suited to showcase Kartadzinu's distinctive features:

  • Participles as predicates: The many "I am the [one who]..." statements use Kartadzinu's extensive participle system
  • Cleft constructions: For emphasis (E ela ke... "It is she who...")
  • Construct state: For divine titles and intimate relationships
  • Semantic shifts: sapere (experiential knowing) vs konesere (intellectual knowing)
  • VSO word order: Elevated register throughout befitting divine speech
  • Telicity markers: ja (already), aset (still) for states of being

The English text follows George W. MacRae's translation from the Coptic.

I. The Invocation / La Invokatsione

English

I was sent forth from the power,
and I have come to those who reflect upon me,
and I have been found among those who seek after me.
Look upon me, you who reflect upon me,
and you hearers, hear me.
You who are waiting for me, take me to yourselves.
And do not banish me from your sight.
And do not make your voice hate me, nor your hearing.
Do not be ignorant of me anywhere or any time. Be on your guard!
Do not be ignorant of me.

Kartadzinu

Mandata fui de la potensa,
e vinai a kelus ke refletan su me,
e trovata fui inter kelus kerkantes me.
Regardate me, vos reflektantes su me,
e vos oditores, odite me.
Vos aspetantes me, prendite me a vos.
E non banite me de vista-vostra.
E non fazite ke vots-vostra odie me, ni oditsa-vostra.
Non siets ignorantes de me en nulu loku o nulu tempu. State en gwardia!
Non siets ignorantes de me.

I am the mother of my father
and the sister of my husband

And he is my offspring in (due) time,
and my power is from him.
I am the staff of his power in his youth,
and he is the rod of my old age.
And whatever he wills happens to me.
I am the silence that is incomprehensible
and the idea whose remembrance is frequent.
I am the voice whose sound is manifold
and the word whose appearance is multiple.
I am the utterance of my name.

III. The Challenge to Listeners / La Desfida a lus Oditores

English

Why, you who hate me, do you love me,
and hate those who love me?
You who deny me, confess me,
and you who confess me, deny me.
You who tell the truth about me, lie about me,
and you who have lied about me, tell the truth about me.
You who know me, be ignorant of me,
and those who have not known me, let them know me.

Kartadzinu

Porke, vos ke odiats me, amats me,
e odiats kelus ke aman me?
Vos ke negats me, konfessate me,
e vos ke konfessats me, negate me.
Vos ke disits veritate de me, mentite de me,
e vos ke ja mentivistis de me, disite veritate de me.
Vos ke konesits me, siets ignorantes de me,
e kelus ke non ja konesiveru me, ke konesan me.

V. Compassion and Cruelty / Kompasione e Krudeletate

English

But I, I am compassionate and I am cruel.
Be on your guard!

Do not hate my obedience
and do not love my self-control.
In my weakness, do not forsake me,

For why do you despise my fear

I am she who is weak,

I, I am godless,

Translation Notes

Features Showcased

1. Participles as Predicates

Throughout the text, participles function as main predicates rather than just modifiers:

  • la onorata e la despresata "the honored one and the scorned one"
  • kelus kerkantes me "those seeking me"
  • vos reflektantes "you reflecting"

2. Construct State

Used for intimate/inalienable relationships:

  • potensa-mea "my power" (power-my)
  • nóme-meu "my name"
  • substantsa-mea "my substance"

3. VSO Word Order

The elevated divine register uses VSO throughout:

  • Mandata fui de la potensa "Sent was-I from the power"
  • Trovata fui "Found was-I"

4. Telicity Markers

  • ja marks completed states: ja fue odiata "has been hated"
  • ja-mais (never again): non moriranu ja-mais "they will not die ever-again"

5. Semantic Distinctions

  • konesere (intellectual knowing): vos ke konesits me "you who know me"
  • sapere would be used for experiential/tasting knowledge (not applicable here)
  • parabula for divine speech/word (not just vots)

6. Demonstrative System

  • kelu/kela/kelus/kelas for distal reference to abstract/divine concepts
  • istu for immediate/present reference
